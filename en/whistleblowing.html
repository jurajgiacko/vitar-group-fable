Whistleblowing

The internal reporting system of VITAR, s.r.o. under Czech Act No. 171/2023 Coll., on the protection of whistleblowers.

Designated person: Bc. Pavla Polášková · whistleblowing@vitar.cz
Written reports: VITAR, s.r.o., třída Tomáše Bati 385, 763 02 Zlín, Czech Republic

Who can file a report

A whistleblower is a person working for VITAR, s.r.o. (Company ID: 00566632) under an employment contract or work agreement. A report concerns possible unlawful conduct that constitutes a criminal offence, an administrative offence with a maximum fine of at least CZK 100,000, or a breach of the whistleblower act or other Czech or EU legislation.

How to file a report

  • in writing — by post to the company address (třída Tomáše Bati 385, 763 02 Zlín), or by e-mail to the designated person,
  • orally — in a personal meeting with the designated person,
  • combined — in writing and orally,
  • or by filing with the Czech Ministry of Justice.

State your name, surname, date of birth and a contact address (postal or e-mail). The company does not accept anonymous reports.

What protection you are entitled to

  • A ban on any retaliation (dismissal, demotion, pay reduction, etc.) and on threats of it — the protection also covers persons close to the whistleblower.
  • Strict confidentiality of the identity of the whistleblower and of persons named in the report.
  • Acknowledgement of receipt within 7 days, outcome notification within 30 days (extendable in complex cases, at most twice).

Protection does not apply to knowingly false reports — these carry a fine of up to CZK 50,000.

Legal framework

Czech Act No. 171/2023 Coll. on whistleblower protection and Directive (EU) 2019/1937 on the protection of persons who report breaches of Union law. The internal directive has been effective since 1 August 2023. Report records are kept for 5 years and are accessible only to the designated person.
